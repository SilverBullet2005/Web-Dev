Very-large-scale integration is the process of creating an integrated circuit by combining millions or billions of MOSFET transistors onto a single chip. VLSI began in the 1970s when MOS integrated circuit chips were developed and then widely adopted, enabling complex semiconductor and telecommunication technologies.
